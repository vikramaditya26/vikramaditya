Finance Notes

1. Saving & Emergency Fund

Before investing, build a strong financial foundation. Start by saving at least 3–6 months of living expenses in an emergency fund. Keep this in a safe place like a savings account or liquid mutual fund, not in risky investments.

  • Track your monthly expenses → use a simple notebook or apps like Moneycontrol.
  • Save before you spend → follow the 50-30-20 rule (Needs 50%, Wants 30%, Savings 20%).

2. Budgeting

A budget is simply a plan for your money. Warren Buffett says, "Do not save what is left after spending, but spend what is left after saving."

  • Note down monthly income and expenses.
  • Avoid lifestyle inflation (don’t upgrade everything when salary increases).
  • Use tools: Zerodha Varsity or GoodBudget.

3. Basics of Investing

Once savings and budget are set, move to investments. Charlie Munger reminds us, "The big money is not in the buying and selling, but in the waiting."

  • Stocks: Ownership in companies. High return, high risk.
  • Mutual Funds: Professionals manage your money. Safer than individual stock picking.
  • Index Funds/ETFs: Low-cost, passive funds that track the market (e.g., Nifty 50).
  • Always invest with a long-term mindset (5–10+ years).

Learn more about Index Funds

4. Insurance & Protection

Insurance protects you from financial disasters. Nikhil Kamath (Zerodha co-founder) often stresses on managing risk before chasing returns.

  • Health Insurance: Essential to protect from hospital bills.
  • Term Life Insurance: If you have dependents, get pure term life cover (not expensive plans with investment).
  • Avoid mixing insurance with investment products (like ULIPs).

5. Continue Learning

Finance is a lifelong skill. Keep learning a little every day. Here are some trusted resources:

  • Zerodha Varsity (Free courses)
  • Pranjal Kamra YouTube
  • Books: The Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham, Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki.
