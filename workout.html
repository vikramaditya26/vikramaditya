Workout

Consistency Over Everything

Fitness is not about doing crazy heavy workouts once in a while. It’s about showing up, week after week, and staying consistent. Even a simple routine done regularly will transform your body and confidence. The key: Don’t chase perfection, chase progress.

Push Day (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

Push workouts train the muscles that push weight away from your body. Here are some beginner-friendly exercises:

  • Push-ups – Classic bodyweight move to build chest and triceps.
  • Incline Push-ups – Easier version if you’re starting out.
  • Dumbbell Shoulder Press – Strengthens shoulders and triceps.
  • Bench Press – Great for chest growth (use light weight first).
  • Tricep Dips – Builds arm strength and definition.

Pull Day (Back, Biceps)

Pull workouts strengthen your back and arms. They improve posture and give that “V-shape” look .

  • Pull-ups – Best for back and biceps (start with assisted if needed).
  • Dumbbell Rows – Builds mid-back thickness.
  • Bicep Curls – Simple and effective for arm growth.
  • Face Pulls – Great for shoulder health.
  • Lat Pulldown – Perfect beginner substitute for pull-ups.

Leg Day (Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes, Calves)

Never skip leg day! Strong legs improve your athletic ability and overall balance.

  • Bodyweight Squats – Start simple and focus on form.
  • Lunges – Build balance and strength.
  • Romanian Deadlifts – Strengthens hamstrings and glutes.
  • Calf Raises – For strong and defined calves.
  • Leg Press – A safe way to add weight for beginners.

Diet for Skinny People (Gaining Weight the Right Way)

If you’re naturally skinny, gaining muscle is about eating more than you burn while keeping the food quality high. Here are simple rules:

  • Eat in a Calorie Surplus – Track your meals. Add an extra 400–500 calories daily.
  • Protein is King – Paneer, lentils, whey protein shakes.
  • Carbs for Energy – Rice, roti, oats, potatoes, bananas – don’t fear carbs.
  • Healthy Fats – Nuts, peanut butter, ghee, avocados keep hormones strong.
  • Eat Frequently – 5–6 smaller meals work better than 2 huge meals.
  • Train & Rest – Muscles grow when you sleep, not just in the gym.

Bonus tip: Don’t try to bulk up with just junk food. You’ll only gain fat, not muscle. Stick to whole foods, train hard, and watch yourself grow stronger.
